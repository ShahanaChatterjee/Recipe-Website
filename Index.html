Bong-Thai Coconut Fish Curry

Flavor Profile

A creamy fish curry falvored with eastern spices, paired with fried eggplants, crispy plantain-potato cakes and pandan rice.

Ingredients

For the Curry:

  • 2 Indian carp steaks
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1 small onion, finely sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tbsp Thai red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (optional)
  • 1-inch ginger piece, julienned
  • 1 stalk lemongrass
  • 1 kaffir lime leaf (optional)
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

For the Hash:

  • 1 raw plantain, peeled and grated
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and grated
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Mustard oil for shallow frying

For the Fried Eggplant:

  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Mustard oil for shallow frying

For the Rice:

  • 1 cup short-grain white rice
  • 2 cups water
  • Pandan leaf (optional)
  • Salt

Method

Rice Preparation:

  1. Rinse rice thoroughly. Boil with water and pandan leaf if using.
  2. Simmer covered until water is absorbed (around 15 minutes).

Plantain-Potato Hash:

  1. Grate plantain and potato, squeeze excess water.
  2. Mix with nigella seeds, chopped chili, and salt.
  3. Shape into cakes and shallow fry until golden.

Fried Eggplants:

  1. Sprinkle the eggplant slices with turmeric and salt. Let sit for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat mustard oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Fry the eggplant slices until golden brown on both sides.
  4. Drain on paper towels and keep warm.

Fish Curry:

  1. Marinate fish with salt and turmeric for 10 mins.
  2. Fry fish in mustard oil until golden, set aside.
  3. Temper mustard seeds, saute onions, ginger, chilies, lemongrass, and lime leaf.
  4. Add curry paste, coconut milk, and simmer.
  5. Add fish steaks, simmer for 8-10 minutes, garnish with coriander.
